Pani Puri

Description

Pani Puri, also known as Golgappa, is a popular Indian street food consisting of crispy puris filled with spicy and tangy water, tamarind chutney, and a mixture of mashed potatoes and chickpeas. It's a delightful burst of flavors in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 20-30 puris (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala
  • 1/2 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup tamarind chutney
  • 1/2 cup mint-coriander chutney
  • For the pani (spiced water):
    • 1 cup mint leaves
    • 1 cup coriander leaves
    • 2-3 green chilies
    • 1-inch piece of ginger
    • 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
    • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
    • 1 tsp chaat masala
    • 1/2 tsp black salt
    • Salt to taste
    • 4 cups cold water

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, chopped cilantro, chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
  2. For the pani (spiced water), grind the mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, and ginger into a smooth paste using a little water.
  3. In a large bowl, add the prepared paste, tamarind pulp, roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, and regular salt. Mix well.
  4. Add 4 cups of cold water to the mixture and stir. Adjust salt and spices to taste. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.
  5. To serve, gently make a small hole in the center of each puri.
  6. Fill each puri with a spoonful of the potato-chickpea mixture.
  7. Add a small amount of tamarind chutney and mint-coriander chutney into each puri.
  8. Finally, dip each filled puri into the prepared pani (spiced water) and enjoy immediately.
